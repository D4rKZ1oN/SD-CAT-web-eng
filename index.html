In El Salvador, air quality is affected by various sources of pollution, such as vehicle exhaust emissions, waste burning, and industrial activity. In addition, the country has suffered in recent years from forest fires that also contribute to air pollution. It is important to address these challenges through policies and actions to reduce air pollution and improve the quality of life for people in El Salvador and around the world. This includes promoting cleaner technologies and practices, improving public transportation, and encouraging renewable energy and sustainable agriculture. Education and public awareness are also key to promoting changes in behavior and decision making that support a healthier environment.

0-50 Good Air quality is considered satisfactory and air pollution poses little or no risk. None
51-100 Moderate Air quality is acceptable; however, for some pollutants there may be a moderate health concern for a very small number of people who are particularly sensitive to air pollution. Active children and adults, as well as people with respiratory diseases such as asthma, should limit prolonged outdoor activity.
101-150 Unhealthy for sensitive groups Members of sensitive groups may experience health effects. The general public is not likely to be affected. Active children and adults, as well as people with respiratory diseases such as asthma, should limit prolonged outdoor activity.
151-200 Unhealthy Everyone may begin to experience health effects; members of sensitive groups may experience more severe health effects. Active children and adults, as well as people with respiratory diseases such as asthma, should avoid prolonged outdoor activity; all others, especially children, should limit prolonged outdoor activity.
201-300 Very unhealthy Health warnings under emergency conditions. It is more likely that the entire population will be affected. Active children and adults, as well as people with respiratory diseases such as asthma, should avoid all outdoor activity; all others, especially children, should limit outdoor activity.
300+ Dangerous Health alert: all may experience more serious health effects Everyone should avoid all outdoor activity.
